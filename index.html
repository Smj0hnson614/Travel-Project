Why Turks and Caicos?

Turks and Caicos, home to prestine beaches and the third largest barrier reef in the world

The Princess Alexandra National Park is an extensive and important protected area in the Turks and Caicos that includes attractions such as Grace Bay Beach, the Bight Beach, the Bight Reef (also known as Coral Gardens), Smith’s Reef, Leeward Beach, Mangrove Cay, and Little Water Cay.

Also inside the national park is the barrier reef that protects Grace Bay, which features beautiful snorkeling and scuba diving locations. One of the top things to do when on vacation in the Turks and Caicos is to snorkel on this amazing barrier reef.

Restaurants

Best restaurants in Turks and Caicos

Da Conch Shack

A popular open-air beachfront restaurant specializing in locally harvested conch, seafood, and Caribbean dishes including jerk chicken and ribs. Local bands play every Raggae Wednesday. House-made desserts include Key Lime Pie, Ginger Cheesecake, and Rum Cake. Family and group friendly

Address:

Blue Hills Rd, TKCA 1ZZ, Turks and Caicos Islands

Why visit Da Conch Shack

One of the greatest attractions of Da Conch Shack is it's beach location and al fresco dining, complete with coconut trees and picnic tables in the sand.

Learn more

Omar's Beach Hut

Omar’s Beach Hut is located on the picturesque turquoise waters of Five Cays Beach, and offers the tantalizing option of dining on picnic tables in the shallow water fronting the restaurant. For those wanting a break from the sun, there’s also the option of tables in the shade. Omar’s serves a menu of popular Turks and Caicos and Caribbean dishes, with ample local seafood and jerk options.

Address:

Five Cays TKCA 1ZZ, Turks and Caicos Islands

Why visit Omar’s Beach Hut

The restaurant's specialty is fresh seafood (from the local fishermen’s dock next door) and jerk dishes, and prides itself on great service and prices, with a relaxing and quiet atmosphere.

Learn more

Turks Kebab

Turks Kebab is a casual restaurant in Grace Bay that serves Turkish, Mediterranean, and Greek fare, at a decent price. Dishes are made to order and incorporate fresh produce, which makes the restaurant a popular choice with locals and visitors alike. The establishment is also the only place serving true doner kebab in the Turks and Caicos.

Address:

Allegro Rd, Grace Bay TKCA 1ZZ, Turks & Caicos Islands

Why visit Turks Kebabas

One of the greatest attractions of Turks Kebab is pide pizza, a unique flatbread style. Choose from classics such as cheese and pepperoni, as well as more-developed flavors, including a Kusbasili meat pizza, Mediterranean, and vegetarian pies. As they are influenced by Turkish pide flat bread, the restaurant’s pizzas have an unusual boat shape, which allows for convenient portions.
